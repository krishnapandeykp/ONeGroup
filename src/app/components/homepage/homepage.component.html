Organic Nano electronics (ONe) Group is dedicated to cutting-edge interdisciplinary research in the expansive realm of organic and polymer-based devices. Our endeavors encompass a wide spectrum of activities, from the synthesis of high-performance materials such as conducting polymers, nano-composites, and nano-structured materials, to a comprehensive exploration of their fundamental properties. These materials are meticulously examined with a view to their potential applications in cutting-edge fields like Organic Photovoltaics (OPVs), Organic Field-Effect Transistors (OFETs), Sensors, and Actuators.

About Us

Our Work: Within the realm of organic electronics, our collective efforts encompass the intricate synthesis of conducting polymers, nanocomposites, and nano-structured materials. These materials find their application in a wide array of organic electronic devices, including organic photovoltaics, sensors, field-effect transistors, and electromagnetic interference (EMI) shielding solutions.

Sensors: We specialize in the design, synthesis, and fabrication of molecules tailored for sensor applications. These sensors are developed to detect a diverse range of elements and compounds, such as volatile organic compounds (VOCs), heavy metal ions, nitrates, and various bio-organisms.

Molecular Design and Synthesis: Our team is deeply engaged in the field of molecular design and synthesis. We meticulously engineer molecules with precisely tuned band-gaps to fulfill a variety of organic electronic applications. These applications include photovoltaics, field-effect transistors, sensors, and EMI shielding technologies.

Device Architecturing: We specialize in various aspects of device architecture. Our skill set includes nano-structuring techniques like electrospinning and non-lithography, ternary blending, and the development of organic-inorganic hybrid materials. We also explore the fascinating world of up-conversion materials and undertake large-area studies focused on understanding the structure-property relationship, charge transport properties, electrode-semiconductor interfaces, device failure analysis, and aging mechanisms.

Packaging: We are dedicated to designing and synthesizing ultra-low permeable monolithic encapsulations. Additionally, we are involved in the development of cutting-edge gas permeability measurement systems to ensure the longevity and performance of our devices.
